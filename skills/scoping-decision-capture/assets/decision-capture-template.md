# <Feature / Notebook> Decision Capture

## 1. Core Problem Or Question

State the central problem, opportunity, or scientific question in one or two sentences.

Then capture:

- why this matters
- who it matters to
- what insight, capability, or outcome the project enables
- why this approach is meaningful in the broader InsightFold, AFDB, PDBe, or structural-biology context

## 2. How The System Works

Summarize:

- core operational logic
- important system components
- key algorithms, models, or processes
- major inputs and outputs
- critical metrics or signals
- assumptions the system relies on
- known limitations or weaknesses discussed

## 3. Constraints, Caveats, And Reliability

For each important caveat, record:

- why it matters
- how it should be communicated to users
- whether mitigation strategies were discussed

## 4. Scope Decisions

For each major decision, state:

- what was decided
- why it was decided
- what tradeoff was accepted

Use phrasing like: `We decided X because Y.`

## 5. User Journey / Narrative Flow

For each stage, capture:

- what the user does
- what the system does
- what the user sees
- what the user learns or accomplishes
- where interpretation or decision-making occurs
- where trust, explanation, or context is important

## 6. Open Questions And Deferred Decisions

For each unresolved item, include:

- the question
- why it was deferred
- what information or validation would resolve it
- constraints affecting the decision

## 7. Success Criteria / Graduation Signal

Capture what would indicate the idea is successful enough to:

- scale further
- become a standing notebook
- justify additional investment
- integrate into AFDB, PDBe, or the InsightFold database
- automate, precompute, or operationalize

Also capture stop or reconsideration signals.
